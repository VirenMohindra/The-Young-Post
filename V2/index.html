Who We Are.

We believe that, as stewards of the future, today’s young people desire a mature news and information source that provides a bold flavour to our generation. Today’s young people are hungry for information, and the post aims to ride on the wave of that inquisition – providing news and analysis of the latest political, technological, economic, and social events that are shaping the world.

A Young Voice

The Young Post lays emphasis on youth generated investigative journalism and mature analysis of events and people that are making a mark on the world today. We believe in providing a fresh perspective - a young perspective - to global questions and issues today.

Made with Love

The Young Post is designed, constructed, and forged by none other than the finest websmiths and developers - to provide a user centric experience that is free of ads, paywalls, and other obstacles to information.

News on a Global Scale

With an editor and accompanying staff on nearly every continent - The Young Post aims to deliver a truly global platter of news and analysis for readers to devour.

Our Mission

We want to set a new benchmark in youth journalism, and show the world that young people are indeed capable of having their voices heard in a society that is anything but receptive of their voices.

Our Principles.

What sets The Young Post apart?

Youth Powered

The most important thing to know about The Young Post is that we are a youth fuelled and driven organisation that prides itself in the capability and achievements of the young people who form our ranks.

Grassroots and Long Branches

The Young Post aims to combine a grassroots approach to journalism with a global scope and ambition in mind, fusing the size of a global enterprise with the originality of a local daily. We believe in amalgamating local perspectives with global audiences.

Open and Liberal

The Young Post doesn’t have an agenda to push, and neither does it affiliate itself with any group. We hope this becomes evident from our work. Smearing and slander aren’t a part of our modus operandi. The news and the truth are what matter at TYP.

Mature Journalism

Unlike so many other youth publications – The Young Post intends to be a mature news source for an inquisitive and information hungry generation, not a poetry and short story depot. We lay a strong emphasis on investigative journalism and intellectual analysis.

Stories that Matter

The Young Post seeks to bring to light stories that matter to young people today – as well as serve as both a depository and source of information to millennials and teenagers as they discover themselves and the world around them.

Immersive User Experience

The Young Post is crafted with its audience in mind and will aspire to provide an engaging, polished, and deeply immersive reader experience, fashioned by highly experienced hands, to ensure that we serve as the go to news and opinion source for young people today.

A Global Community.

The Young Post bonds a local focus with a global outreach, with staff on almost every continent.

Meet The Family.

A group of passionate individuals - meet the world's most prestigious youth editorial board

Aditya Karkera

Editor-in-Chief

Aditya Karkera is a firm advocate of youth initiative. Having written for The Huffington Post, the Times of India, and the Times of Israel, Aditya also serves as Director-General of YouthCorp - a United Nations MGCY member organisation.

Arnav Agarwal

Eastern Executive Editor

A debate geek with a zest for journalism, Arnav has served as a school reporter for the BBC, written for the Times of India, edited the Scindia Review, and studies at Princeton University.

Emma Norris

Editor - Europe

An avid exponent of change, Emma leads the JS Mill Society - which has hosted the likes of UK Cabinet Ministers, media luminaries and new age entrepreneurs - while having worked at ARM plc and VC Advent Life Sciences.

Jaehyun Park

Editor - East Asia

An infallible champion of youth policy. Studying at Harvard University, Jaehyun serves as founder and adviser to Young Homo Politicus - one of the largest youth organisations in South Korea.

Jordan Kindler

Editor - North America

Jordan Kindler is someone who values social equity. Studying at Yale University, he has served as editor in chief of The Podium, is a laureate of the Virginia Finkel Pierce Scholarship in the humanities, and has worked with a trading firm on Wall Street.

Peter Russell

Western Executive Editor

A man of varied interests, he has several patents pending, is the 2013 International Sustainable World Project Olympiad Grand Award winner, and sits on the staff of the National High School Journal of Science. Peter studies at Princeton University.

Armaan Grewal

Editor - South Asia

Armaan Grewal is not someone who "toes the line". Studying at Brown University, Armaan has a rich journalistic experience, having worked with CNN-IBN's Parikshit Luthra and Rajdeep Sardesai as a Journalist intern, and written for Amalgamated.

Viren Mohindra

Chief Technical Officer

A tech connoisseur, Viren Mohindra studies film at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts, is a Co-Founder of Nuked Studios, has been awarded fellowships, and has been interviewed by online and print media.

Hemangini Raina

Editor - Middle East

Defined by her drive, Hemangini is a budding social entrepreneur. She is attending Stanford University, having written a regular column for Bahrain’s Gulf Weekly and serving as the Editor-in-chief of her high school’s literary magazine.
